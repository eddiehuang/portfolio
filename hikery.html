Hikery

In Spring 2013, I took a graduate-level class on User Research. Every semester, we are paired off with a client for our course project. In this course, we are supposed to implement the techniques we've learned throughout the semester. Our client was The Hikery, a web application for bringing people together through hiking.

View Report

Project Goals

  • Investigate factors that motivate or deter people from hiking.
  • Determine what attributes of a hike are most conducive to socializing.
  • Identify the best process to enable a good socialization experience through hiking.

Research Methods

  • Usability testing with interviews and interface tour (8 participants)
  • Competitive analysis (6 direct and indirect competitors)
  • Online and in-person survey (87 responses)

Key Findings

  • Safety is more of a concern for females than for males.
  • Users already have other preferred ways for planning activities.
  • People enjoy spending time with individuals with shared interests.
  • Users prefer to socialize with new people outdoors more than they currently do.
  • Navigation bar is unclear.
  • People like to get to know others in group settings.
  • Hiking is more suited for getting to know people better rather than meeting them for the first time.
  • The datepicker is not intuitive and confuses most of the users.
  • Users are not informed once they have signed up.
  • Users want to preview key features of the application before signing up.
  • The application does not provide users enough contextual information about hiking destinations.
  • Active hikers are more open to meeting new people through hiking.
